Packing Panic

  • Prev
  • Next
  • View Itch.io page
  • View Video

Packing panic was a game created for a university game jam where we had to work in a team to create a game within four days. In the game, you must quickly drag and drop food items into your lunch box before time is up. Every time you complete a round, you are given less time. However, you are awarded one extra second if you can accurately place the fruit in the correct place and not just throw them in the box. The player loses if they run out of time. Ironically, the trick to getting the highest points and winning more levels is not to panic when playing this game.

This game was judged by several lectures at my University and a game developer from Codemasters. This game won the competition.

Roles & Responsibilities

Role Responsibilities
Gameplay programmer

Since I was the only programmer on the project and we did not have much time, I had to make sure we could quickly iterate the prototype and stick to our design. We tried many prototypes of different game ideas and eventually came up with Packing panic.
Games designer

We all agreed to contribute to the game's design as a team. While programming the game, I would add mechanics to see if it made it more fun or exciting. I suggested that the game be competitive and that the player quickly drags and drops items to score points.

Some notes about the project

  • Made in Unity.
  • We used royalty-free assets such as art, music and sound effects from royalty-free sites. However, I did animate the title screen animation and a few sprites, such as the notepad were created by our team.
