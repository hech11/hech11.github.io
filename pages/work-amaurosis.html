Amaurosis

  • Prev
  • Next
  • View Video

Amaurosis was a university project where we simulated a small team of programmers to create this game. I was the main programming lead and oversaw development of nearly all systems. I was also responsible for shader work and architecturing the game.

Amaurosis is a first-person horror survival game where the player you must complete objectives while evading a monster that is roaming around and hunting you. Both player and monster are blind.

For the player to see their surroundings they must make noise to emit noise waves around them. The player can make noise by walking and throwing objects. However while noise waves are used to help the player see they are also used by the monster to help locate the player. To win players must complete mini games and turn on generators to power the door to escape.

Some notes about the project

  • Made entirely in C++.
  • A batch renderer.
  • Audio system. Including spatial audio
  • Physics.
  • ECS system.
  • Can script entities via CSharp classes.
  • An editor. Including a scene and game viewer, hierarchy and component inspector and more.
  • Can save and load scenes.
